Severe Vomitting

During the first trimester of pregnancy, many women experience the bouts of nausea and vomiting known as morning sickness. It usually begins around the 6th week of pregnancy, peaks around week 9, and disappears by weeks 16 to 18. But what's not normal is when morning sickness becomes so severe that a woman persistently vomits several times a day, loses weight, and becomes dehydrated or at risk for dehydration.

Call the Doctor Immediately, if you have the following symptoms

Brownish color vomit with blood

Weight loss

Fainting or dizziness

Decreased urination

Rapid heart rate

Following treatments will be helpful but not harmful

consuming a bland diet

eating frequent small meals

avoiding spicy and fatty foods

drinking plenty of fluids

eating high-protein snacks
